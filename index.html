What is an ACL injury and why is it happening so frequently in womens football?

Read time: 5 minutes

To understand why ACL injuries are such an issue in womens football we should first understand the injury.

What is an ACL?

There are diffent systems that make up the human body. Two of these systems are are going to be talked about in relation to ACL injuries. These two systems are Skeletal and Muscular, the muscular system is what connects the bones of our skeletal system together and enable us to move. ACL stands for Anterior Cruciate Ligament, it is the ligiment that is loacted in your knee. This ligmanet allows for a bone called the patella or your knee cap to be connected to your Femur which is your thigh bone and Tibia which is your shin bone. This ligament is genrally able to hold up under a lot of stress day to day however for athletes who push their limits in their chosen sports it can lead to them having a ACL injury. This can happen due to more pressure being put through the knee joint at one time, any sudden movement a athlete may make or them stopping quickly, resulting in the ACL either tearing or snapping and putting the althete in a tremendous amount of pain. This is an injury that is becoming more common place in womens football as the womens game becomes more high profile, the amount of games players are playing increases and rest time decreases. Injuries obviously are not a new consept in sport or in women's football in particular, while we as human being are simular in basic structure there are many physiclogial differences between men and women. Periods can have a massive impact on a womans ablitly to perform at the highest level, and there have been teams within English Womens Football that have looked into this impact and how they can best mangae it so it has less of a detrimantal effect on the players performances.

What is the recovery time from a ACL injury?

the time frame for recovering from an ACL injury can be between six and nine months, however for athletes this is signifinactly longer increasing to between nine and twelve months before they are avised to play a competiave match.
